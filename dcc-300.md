# Course Design: Weather Station

This project teaches students how to capture environmental data and present it in a readable format. By combining a digital sensor with a visual display, students learn the full cycle of data acquisition: reading raw signals, processing them into meaningful units, and outputting them to a user interface.

---

### Course Goal

To provide students with a foundational understanding of data visualization and sensor-to-display communication. The goal is to build a functional, desktop-ready weather station that translates invisible environmental variables into human-readable information.

---

### What Students Will Learn

* **I2C Communication:** Understanding the Inter-Integrated Circuit protocol, which allows the ESP32 to talk to the OLED screen using only two wires.
* **Sensor Polling:** Reading data from the DHT22 sensor, including handling timing requirements to ensure accurate temperature and humidity readings.
* **Data Calibration & Mapping:** Converting raw sensor output into Celsius/Fahrenheit and percentages, and ensuring the data is stable before display.
* **Display Programming:** Managing a pixel-based OLED screen—drawing text, clearing the screen buffer, and updating values dynamically.
* **System Integration:** Combining inputs (sensor) and outputs (screen) into a single, cohesive main loop.

---

### List Items Needed

* **Microcontroller:** ESP32 development board.
* **Sensor:** DHT22 temperature and humidity sensor.
* **Display:** 0.96" I2C OLED display (SSD1306).
* **Prototyping:** Breadboard and jumper wires.
* **Development Environment:** Laptop with Arduino IDE or PlatformIO.
* **Power Source:** USB cable (for programming and power).

---

### Example Usage & Applications

* **Smart Home Climate Monitor:** A desktop device to track the comfort levels of a bedroom or home office.
* **Greenhouse Controller:** A monitoring unit for small indoor plant setups to ensure optimal humidity.
* **Data Logging Station:** A foundation for adding an SD card module to save weather history over long periods.
* **Portable Environmental Scout:** A battery-powered tool to compare air quality or climate between different rooms in a building.
